Chapter 01

The Season By The Numbers

The 2024 Dodgers delivered one of the most dominant seasons in modern baseball history. A 98-win regular season, a World Series championship over the New York Yankees in 5 games, and a roster featuring three MVPs — Shohei Ohtani, Mookie Betts, and Freddie Freeman — made Dodger Stadium the most coveted ticket in all of professional sports last year.

The numbers back it up. The Dodgers led all of MLB with an average paid attendance of 49,067 — nearly 20% higher than the second-ranked Yankees. Their gate revenue hit $4.29 million per home game, the highest in baseball. And their average ticket price reached $87, reflecting the full weight of Ohtani mania, sustained winning, and a fanbase that showed up in record numbers every single night.

Chapter 02

What Your Seat Was Actually Worth

Dodgers season ticket holders sit on 81 home games per season. The average holder misses 15–25 of those. At a median resale price of $177 per ticket on the secondary market in 2024 — and significantly more for premium matchups — missing even a handful of games without a resale partner represents thousands of dollars in lost revenue per seat.

High-demand games told an even bigger story. Opening Day tickets sold for $604+ on the secondary market. Yankees rematch games at Dodger Stadium were fetching $414–$436. Rivalry and playoff-push games regularly traded at 3–5x face value. For holders who sat on unused tickets, that money simply evaporated.

A two-seat Dodgers holder missing 20 games at median resale value left over $7,000 on the table in 2024. That's before you factor in premium game pricing.

Chapter 04

Why 2025 Is the Hottest Dodgers Resale Season Ever

Everything that made 2024 a premium resale market is amplified in 2025. The Dodgers enter the season as defending World Series champions and the clear Vegas favorite to repeat at +240. Shohei Ohtani is returning as NL MVP and now pitching again. Average Dodgers ticket prices for 2025 have already jumped 25% year-over-year — the second-largest increase in all of MLB — reaching $181 as the new average.

The premium game calendar alone tells the story: Opening Day against the Tigers is averaging $604 on resale. Back-to-back Yankees rematch games in June are sitting at $436 and $414. These are not outliers — they are the new floor for marquee Dodgers matchups. For season ticket holders, 2025 isn't just an opportunity. It's the biggest one you've ever had.
